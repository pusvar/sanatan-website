Fasts & Festivals

Monthly Hindu Calendar, Fasts, and Auspicious Muhurats

July 2026

July 25, 2026

Devshayani Ekadashi

Marks the beginning of Chaturmas. Lord Vishnu goes into a state of deep meditation.

July 29, 2026

Guru Purnima

A sacred day to honor spiritual and academic teachers (Gurus) and seek their divine blessings.

July 30, 2026

Shravan Begins (Sawan)

The holy month of Shravan begins. Mondays are highly auspicious for worshipping Lord Shiva.

Book Rudrabhishek

August 2026

August 14, 2026

Nag Panchami

Traditional worship of snakes or serpents observed by Hindus.

August 28, 2026

Raksha Bandhan

Celebrates the bond of love and protection between brothers and sisters on Shravan Purnima.

September 2026

September 4, 2026

Krishna Janmashtami

Grand celebration marking the divine birth of Lord Krishna.

September 14, 2026

Ganesh Chaturthi

Arrival of Lord Ganesha to earth, celebrated with elaborate Pujas.

September 27, 2026

Pitru Paksha Begins

A 16-day lunar period when Hindus pay homage to their ancestors through Shradh Karma.

Book Shradh Puja

October 2026

October 11, 2026

Sharad Navratri Begins

Nine nights dedicated to the worship of the divine mother Goddess Durga.

October 20, 2026

Dussehra (Vijayadashami)

Marks the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana, celebrating the triumph of good over evil.

October 31, 2026

Karwa Chauth

A fasting festival where married women pray for the long life of their husbands.

November 2026

November 8, 2026

Diwali (Deepawali)

The festival of lights, celebrating the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya.

November 14, 2026

Chhath Puja

An ancient Vedic festival dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and Chhathi Maiya.

November 24, 2026

Kashi Dev Deepawali

Celebrated on Kartik Purnima. The Ghats of Varanasi are illuminated with millions of diyas to welcome the Gods.

December 2026

December 13, 2026

Vivah Panchami

Observes the divine marriage of Lord Rama and Goddess Sita.

December 19, 2026

Gita Jayanti

Commemorates the day Lord Krishna imparted the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna.

January 2027

January 14, 2027

Makar Sankranti

Dedicated to the Sun God, marking the transition of the sun into Capricorn.

February 2027

February 10, 2027

Vasant Panchami

Dedicated to Goddess Saraswati, marking the preparation for the arrival of spring.

February 15, 2027

Maha Shivaratri

The Great Night of Shiva. A major festival celebrating Lord Shiva.

Book Rudrabhishek

March 2027

March 4, 2027

Holi

The festival of colors, celebrating divine love and the triumph of good over evil.
